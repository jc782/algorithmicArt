Tutorial

Dot-Art uses a photograph, and creates a template that can be used to make a completely custom piece of art. It uses maths previously developed for generating images that had low resolution and limited (8bit) colour options available. For information on the algorithm implemented look here. Dot-Art takes low colour and low resolution to the extreme.

The templates created here are based on just 12 colours. They are the colours easily available here. The artwork is designed for the 6mm stickers, and to fit on an A3 page.

The downloaded template is an A3 page with the required colour sticker marked in each location. A full set of instructions, including the number of required stickers is on the second sheet. The A3 paper allows for a resolutions of 65px x 45px [ie super low resolution]. But even this size will take roughly 6 hours to complete the artwork.

It can be challenging to find good input photos - try things with clear colour blocks and relatively simple shapes. Too much complexity will overwhelm the resolution. Some examples are included below - but I really recommend trying to find a personal photo that works. Perhaps it will make an excellent gift for friends or family.
